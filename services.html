What I Offer

Every journey into conception, birth and parenthood is unique.

When you encounter challenges in your fertility journey and feel dismissed by the mainstream system, I’ll be there with empathy, understanding and evidence-based guidance to support your well-being on the way to conceiving and carrying a healthy pregnancy.

When you feel anxious about where, how, or under what circumstances to give birth, I’ll help you explore your options and make the decisions that are right for YOU. I accompany birth in any setting, fully present with care and attention.

When fear, uncertainty, or discomfort arises during birth, I’ll be there to ease that and champion you in having the birth you envision—supporting you to feel confident and grounded every step of the way.

When people may minimize or dismiss your feelings as you share your birth story, I’ll listen with openness, without judgment and honor your experience with care and respect.

Prenatal Care

Personalized preparation through three in-depth, 2-hour sessions to help you feel informed, grounded and confident as you approach birth.

What’s included:

  • Conversations around your pregnancy, fears, wishes and birth preferences
  • Evidence-based childbirth education (physiology, pain management, positions)
  • Breathing, relaxation and pelvic floor work
  • Birth rights, advocacy and birth plan creation
  • Postpartum preparation, including breastfeeding guidance
  • Optional fascia touch for comfort and optimal baby positioning

Birth Support

Continuous emotional and hands-on care from early labor through the first hours after birth, offering comfort, reassurance and gentle advocacy, so you feel safe, supported and grounded. My role is to help protect your space, support informed decision-making and center your needs and autonomy throughout the birthing process.

What’s included:

  • On-call support and presence from early labor
  • Hands-on comfort measures (massage, counter-pressure, fascia touch, rebozo)
  • Supportive birth environment (lighting, music, essential oils)
  • Continuous presence until 1 to 2 hours after birth
  • Support during initial breastfeeding

Postpartum Care

During the postpartum period, I offer practical and emotional care to support you as you settle into life with your baby. My visits are shaped around your needs, creating space for rest, recovery and open conversation as you navigate this transition.

What’s included:

  • Postnatal visits
  • Breastfeeding guidance to support comfort and milk supply
  • Baby care guidance and practical reassurance
  • Emotional support and space to process your birth experience
  • Caring for your baby so you can rest, shower or take time for yourself
  • Light household help and nourishment support when needed

Fertility Support

A compassionate, evidence-based space for those navigating fertility challenges, while trying to conceive or undergoing IVF, especially when feeling dismissed or unheard by the mainstream system. This package offers emotional support, grounded information and hands-on care to support your well-being on your journey toward conception and a viable pregnancy.

What’s included:

  • Accompaniment over two months
  • Five in-depth sessions
  • Pelvic bodywork to support relaxation and circulation
  • Evidence-based information tailored to your situation
  • Space to explore emotions, fears and hopes without judgment
  • Unlimited phone and SMS support during the accompaniment period

Your questions, answered

I work alongside your medical care, never instead of it.

In Germany, your pregnancy and birth care are led by midwives and, when needed, doctors or hospital teams. As a doula, my role is to offer continuous emotional, physical, and informational support — helping you feel calm, heard, and supported throughout the process.

I collaborate respectfully with midwives and medical staff and can support births in hospitals, birth centers, or at home in Berlin.

Many families choose to contact me during the second trimester, often between weeks 16 and 24. This gives us time to get to know each other and prepare for birth without pressure.

Support can begin at different stages: during pregnancy, during birth or in the postpartum period Some people reach out later in pregnancy or specifically for postpartum support. You’re always welcome to ask — we can see together what feels right and what’s possible.

In Germany, doula support is generally not covered by public health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenkasse).

My work is considered non-medical support and is usually self-paid. Some private or supplementary insurance plans may reimburse parts of the cost, but this varies from provider to provider.

If reimbursement is important to you, I recommend checking directly with your insurance company for clarity.
