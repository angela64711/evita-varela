My story

The birth of my daughter, Zoé, in 2019 marked a profound turning point in my life.

It deepened my understanding of birth as a transformative rite of passage and led me to follow my true calling: supporting families with presence, compassion, and informed care.

As a doula, I support people in advocating for their needs, navigating complex systems and experiencing pregnancy and birth with confidence and autonomy. My role is not to replace medical care, but to complement it—offering continuous emotional presence, practical support and space for informed decision-making throughout pregnancy, birth and the postpartum period.

Before becoming a doula, I worked as a researcher in Biology and Neuroscience. This background continues to shape my work today, grounding my approach in scientific knowledge while honoring the lived, embodied experience of pregnancy. I believe that evidence and intuition are not opposites but can coexist in meaningful and empowering ways.

Having personally navigated PCOS, infertility, pregnancy loss and amenorrhea, I bring both professional knowledge and lived understanding to my work. These experiences have deepened my empathy and reinforced the importance of being truly heard, respected and supported—especially when the medical system feels overwhelming or dismissive.

Living and working in Berlin, I am familiar with the local maternity care landscape and the challenges many people face when navigating it—particularly in a multilingual, international context. I support clients in understanding their options, communicating their preferences and finding a sense of grounding within a system that can often feel impersonal.

I offer doula support in person in Berlin and online. For parts of the year, I am also available in Zurich and Athens.

Your questions, answered

I work alongside your medical care, never instead of it.

In Germany, your pregnancy and birth care are led by midwives and, when needed, doctors or hospital teams. As a doula, my role is to offer continuous emotional, physical, and informational support — helping you feel calm, heard, and supported throughout the process.

I collaborate respectfully with midwives and medical staff and can support births in hospitals, birth centers, or at home in Berlin.

Many families choose to contact me during the second trimester, often between weeks 16 and 24. This gives us time to get to know each other and prepare for birth without pressure.

Support can begin at different stages: during pregnancy, during birth or in the postpartum period Some people reach out later in pregnancy or specifically for postpartum support. You’re always welcome to ask — we can see together what feels right and what’s possible.

In Germany, doula support is generally not covered by public health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenkasse).

My work is considered non-medical support and is usually self-paid. Some private or supplementary insurance plans may reimburse parts of the cost, but this varies from provider to provider.

If reimbursement is important to you, I recommend checking directly with your insurance company for clarity.

As a doula, I offer emotional and physical support, help you prepare and feel informed, support communication and decision-making, provide a calm, steady presence.

I do not provide medical care, perform examinations or procedures, replace midwives or doctors.

This clear separation allows everyone involved to focus on their role, while you feel supported and cared for.
