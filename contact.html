Your questions, answered

I work alongside your medical care, never instead of it.

In Germany, your pregnancy and birth care are led by midwives and, when needed, doctors or hospital teams. As a doula, my role is to offer continuous emotional, physical, and informational support — helping you feel calm, heard, and supported throughout the process.

I collaborate respectfully with midwives and medical staff and can support births in hospitals, birth centers, or at home in Berlin.

Many families choose to contact me during the second trimester, often between weeks 16 and 24. This gives us time to get to know each other and prepare for birth without pressure.

Support can begin at different stages: during pregnancy, during birth or in the postpartum period Some people reach out later in pregnancy or specifically for postpartum support. You’re always welcome to ask — we can see together what feels right and what’s possible.

In Germany, doula support is generally not covered by public health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenkasse).

My work is considered non-medical support and is usually self-paid. Some private or supplementary insurance plans may reimburse parts of the cost, but this varies from provider to provider.

If reimbursement is important to you, I recommend checking directly with your insurance company for clarity.
